# Your Light Card

Your Light corporate card is a convenient way to pay for work expenses directly. Whether you're buying office supplies, traveling for a client meeting, or paying for software subscriptions, your Light card works just like a regular credit card—but with your company's spending oversight.

[Open in Light →](https://app.light.inc/user-cards)

## Activating Your Card

When you first receive your physical Light card, you'll need to activate it before you can use it. Open the Light app, go to **Cards**, select your card, and click **Activate card**. You'll confirm that you have the card in your possession, and the card becomes active right away. Virtual cards don't require activation.

## Viewing Your Card Details

Your full card number, expiration date, and CVV are stored securely in the Light app. You can find this information anytime by:

1. Going to **Cards** in the app
2. Tapping on your active card
3. Revealing the full card number (you may need to authenticate)

If you have a physical card, you can also reveal your 4-digit PIN the same way—it appears in the card details and auto-hides after a few seconds for security.

Keep your card number private and never share it outside of work purchases.

## Checking Your Spending Limit

Your spending limits are set by your finance team (company admins or controllers). Your card can have limits of three types:

- **Per transaction** — the maximum amount for a single purchase
- **Weekly** — the maximum you can spend in a week
- **Monthly** — the maximum you can spend in a month

You can see the limits that apply to your card on your **Cards** page. If you need a limit changed, reach out to your finance team.

## Viewing Recent Transactions

Every purchase you make appears on your Light card immediately. You can see:

- Transaction date and time
- Merchant name and location
- Amount spent
- Receipt status (whether a receipt has been attached)

Browse your recent transactions anytime by tapping **Cards** → your card → **Transactions**. This helps you stay on top of your spending and prepare receipts for your finance team.

## What Happens Next

After you make a purchase, you'll need to attach a receipt to the transaction. This helps your company track spending and ensures accuracy in the books.
